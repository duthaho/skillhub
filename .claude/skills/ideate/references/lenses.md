# Divergence lenses

Each lens is a different direction to push from the frame fixed in Step 0.
Working them in turn is what produces range; a freehand list drifts into
variations of the first idea. Take each applicable lens, force at least one
idea from it, and move on — polishing happens later, not here.

Not every lens fits every frame. Skip a lens only when it genuinely can't
apply, not when it's hard — the hard ones tend to yield the non-obvious
ideas.

## The lenses

1. **Adjacent user** — who *almost* uses this but doesn't quite? Design for
   the neighbor: the beginner next to the expert, the ops team next to the
   developer, the reader next to the writer. What would win them?

2. **Removed constraint** — name the constraint the project currently
   accepts as fixed (needs a config file, assumes one machine, requires an
   account, English-only). Delete it in your head. What becomes possible?

3. **Inversion** — flip the core assumption. If it's pull, make it push. If
   the user drives, let the system drive. If it reports after, make it
   prevent before. Invert the verb in the job sentence.

4. **Form-factor shift** — same value, different surface: CLI ↔ library ↔
   web ↔ chat ↔ scheduled job ↔ IDE extension ↔ API. Where the value lives
   now is rarely the only place it could.

5. **Steal from another domain** — what does a mature field do for this same
   shape of problem? Version control for prose, spam filtering for
   moderation, spaced repetition for onboarding, CI gates for anything. Name
   the field, name the mechanism, port it.

6. **10x / 0.1x** — scale a dimension by an order of magnitude. 10x the
   inputs (does it still hold? what new feature does scale demand?). 0.1x the
   effort/time/setup (what has to disappear to make it that cheap?).

7. **Combine** — fuse two existing pieces of the project, or the project with
   an adjacent tool, into one motion. The value is often in the seam, not
   either part.

8. **Compress the workflow** — watch the user's real sequence of steps to get
   value. Which step can be removed, defaulted, or made automatic? The best
   idea is often a deletion.

9. **Failure-mode mining** — read the top-reacted issues, support questions,
   and "how do I…" threads. Each recurring pain is an idea in negative:
   what feature makes the pain structurally impossible?

10. **Differentiation deepening** — take the project's differentiation axis
    from Step 0 and ask what would make it *twice as true*. Not new
    territory — a moat around the ground already owned.

## Using the lenses well

- **Quota over quality here.** Two mediocre ideas from an unworked lens beat
  one polished idea from the obvious one. Judgment is Step 3's job.
- **A weak idea still counts** — it maps the space and often seeds a strong
  neighbor in clustering.
- **Name the lens beside each idea** in your notes; it makes the Step 2
  clustering and the Step 4 tracker legible, and shows which lenses went
  unworked.
